The Property

Built around the lake, not just beside it.

The water is the living room.

The accordion doors on the main floor open the full width of the house to a covered deck above the lake. Sixteen-foot ceilings, skylights overhead, and unobstructed water views in every direction. Quartz finishes, radiant-heated bathroom floors. The transition between inside and outside is effectively seamless.

A dock built for actual use.

An aluminium gangway leads to a new dock with a log boom that adds natural shelter for swimming and mooring. This is not a decorative float, it is a functional waterfront setup designed around the way people actually use a lake property.

Three decks. Two fire pits. One sandy beach.

The outdoor sequence runs from covered upper deck to a private sandy beach. Three separate covered decks mean the outdoors is usable in shoulder seasons, not just July and August. The fire pits extend evenings well past dark.

Room for everyone without compromise.

Four bedrooms across three levels, each with its own bath, make this a genuinely functional multi-generation or host property. The layout separates the primary suite from guest rooms without sacrificing the flow of the main floor.

Full-time liveable. The infrastructure is there.

Heat pump, radiant floor heating, municipal water and sewer, and a heated detached garage with boat-height clearance. Security system installed. This is not a cabin with cottage-grade infrastructure, it is a home that happens to be on the water.

Location

Youbou. Sunny side of Cowichan Lake.

9646 Creekside is situated between the Town of Lake Cowichan and Youbou. Lake Cowichan is the service centre for the lake. Groceries, restaurants, hardware, and the full range of town amenities are a short drive east. Youbou itself is a quiet residential stretch along the south shore, with a general store and a boat launch. The town is small and low-traffic by design, which is part of the draw. Victoria is roughly 90 minutes by highway.
